Translate This Page Written Communications CUMBERLAND COUNTY DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS The Cumberland County Department of Corrections maintains a mail system in which all incarcerated persons can send and receive mail. For sending mail: All mail, except legal mail from an attorney or law firm, will be searched. Mail should be sent in a self-addressed stamped envelope (S.A.S.E) to the provided address. Books and pictures are allowed, but there are some rules: Books must be softcover and come directly from the authorized publisher. Only 7 pictures, up to a size of 5"x7" each, are allowed, with no nudity. Money can only be sent via United States Postal Service money orders. There's a 3-day hold on deposited money before it's available for use by the incarcerated person. Remember not to send contraband like drugs or sharp objects, as these will be investigated and could lead to prosecution. ****NO COLORED ENVELOPES OR MAGAZINES WILL BE ACCEPTED**** Contact Information Mail should be sent in a self-addressed stamped envelope (S.A.S.E) to the provided address.
