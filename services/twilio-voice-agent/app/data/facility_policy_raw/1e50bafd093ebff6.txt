General Information: The Manson Youth Institution is a level 4 high-security facility. It serves as the Department's primary location for housing accused or sentenced individuals under the age of 22. Recognizing the need to educate youth, staff provide a climate which enhances the development of social skills, while reinforcing ethics, attitude and behavior valued by productive law-abiding citizens. The Manson Youth Institution houses males ranging in age from 15 to 21 in ten separate buildings, each with three wings containing 12 cells, a day room, counselor offices and program room. As the state's only facility for male youth, it houses chronic disciplinary, mental health, high security and general population residents who are involved in a wide variety of programs to include educational and vocational services, as well as addiction treatment. In response to an increase in our young population, a mentoring program was established. Emphasis is placed on development of positive peer relationships, Restorative Justice practices, understanding criminal culture, substance abuse, basic life skills, employment counseling and release planning. The facility is named in honor of John R. Manson, the Department's second Commissioner who served from 1971-1983. He also served as Deputy Commissioner from 1968-1971. Present staffing: 283. PREA Final Report 2021 PREA Final Report 2024 History: The Manson Youth Institution formerly known as the Cheshire Youth Institution opened on October 12, 1982 as a level 4 facility with a population of 360 male residents, ages 15 to 21 situated on 76 acres. On September 17, 1985 the facility was rededicated as the John R. Manson Youth Institution in honor of the late Commissioner John R. Manson. In January of 1986 the facility saw a beginning of doubling of cells, completed on December 1, 1988. In 1994 a 155,000 square foot H.E.T. (Health Education & Training) building opened, containing 22 classrooms, a library, vocational education programming, a full-sized gymnasium, a chapel, mental health and medical facilities, a full production kitchen, a laundry and a warehouse.
