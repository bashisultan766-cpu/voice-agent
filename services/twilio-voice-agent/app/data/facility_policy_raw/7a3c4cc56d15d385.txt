Mailing | Floyd County Sheriff top of page Books & Mailing Mailing a Letter Inmates can send and receive only postcards (with the exception of legal mail). Postcards are available for purchase on the inmate store. ​ You should address the postcards as such: Inmate Name and ID number 925B Peachtree Street NE Box 2062 Atlanta, GA 30309 Emailing Inmates at the Floyd County Sheriff's Office can send/ receive emails through JailATM. Additionally, you can add money to an inmates account through JailATM. Jail ATM Sending Books to a Floyd County Inmate If you’d like to send a book to someone in the Floyd County Jail, please follow these rules: How to Send Books must be ordered online from a trusted site such as Amazon.com or Walmart.com. The book must be shipped directly to the inmate. Address the package like this: ​ Inmate Name and ID number C/O Floyd County Sheriff’s Office 2526 New Calhoun Hwy Rome, Ga 30161 ​ Book Limits Each inmate may have up to 2 books per month. They may only keep 2 books at a time. Inmates may also have 1 religious book, which does not count toward their monthly limit. When an inmate finishes a book and turns it in, it will be placed into their property. Book Requirements Paperback only (no hardcovers). Standard book dimensions (no larger than 8.5" x 11"). No sexually explicit content (no pornography). No content that could threaten jail safety (for example: violence, escape tactics, etc.) Important Note It may take several weeks for a book to arrive at our facility. After arrival, it may take several more days before the book is delivered to the intended inmate. bottom of page
