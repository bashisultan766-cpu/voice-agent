Inmate Mail | Arizona Department of Corrections, Rehabilitation & Reentry Skip to main content Search Search Inmate Mail Inmate Mail Information Digital Mail Scanning The Arizona Department of Corrections, Rehabilitation, and Reentry (ADCRR) is excited to announce that effective December 15, 2025 , we are moving to a digital mail platform for all incoming mail, (with the exception of legal mail, parcels, publications, and official government mail), to help reduce contraband and increase safety and security in our prison complexes. Beginning December 15, 2025 , the above-mentioned mail must be sent to the Digital Mail Processing Center in Texas where it will be sorted, scanned, and uploaded to an inmate’s secure tablet. This mail will be scanned in color and can be saved to an inmate’s tablet to be permanently available for the inmate to enjoy. For inmates that do not have an electronic tablet, or if a tablet malfunctions, the Department will provide access to digital kiosks or will print mail in black and white to ensure incarcerated individuals are able to access their mail. Inmate Legal Mail The legal mail barcode generation app for attorneys and courts and can be found at https://legalmail.azcorrections.gov/ . Please see the application user guide . Centralized Processing For Mail Effective January 07, 2026, the address for sending certain mail items is changing to a new centralized address in Phoenix. While no single effort can completely stop the transmission of dangerous contraband into Arizona’s prisons, ADCRR is committed to implementing every feasible measure to improve the health and safety of our incarcerated population, visitors, and staff. To answer your questions and provide further details about ADCRR’s new mail procedures, we’ve created a list of Frequently Asked Questions . The process for outbound mail has not changed. Support is available 24-hours-a-day, 365-days-a-year by calling Securus® Customer Service at (800) 844-6591 . Transition Grace Period The effective date for sending all incoming mail (other than legal mail, parcels, publications, and official government mail) to the Digital Processing Center in Texas is December 15, 2025 . However, as a courtesy and to help ensure a smooth transition to digital inmate mail, ADCRR is providing a 1-month grace period (December 15, 2025 through January 15, 2026) during which these items– if improperly sent to inmates at prison complexes – will be processed appropriately by ADCRR and provided to inmates. From January 15, 2026 onward, all mail improperly sent to inmates at prison complexes returned to sender .
