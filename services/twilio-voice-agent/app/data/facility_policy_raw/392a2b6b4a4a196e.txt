Website Sign In Search Home Departments Sheriff's Office Divisions Corrections Division (Jail) Inmate Mail & Personal Property Inmate Mail & Personal Property As of April 1, 2026, the Portage County Jail will no longer be accepting physical mail sent to inmates. Mail We do not accept any physical mail sent to the Portage County Jail except for privileged attorney – client mail. All other mail to include any funds should be sent to Reliance. We utilize Reliance Mail Connect at our Facility. They provide a mail scanning service that allows mail to be delivered electronically to inmates. How It Works: When mail is received at Reliance, they scan the pages including the envelope. The scanned file is sent to our facility through Reliance’s Mail Connect software. Within the Relcore system, designated jail staff access Mail Connect to review the content to ensure it is appropriate for release to the inmate. If it is deemed approved, staff will release it and it will be available in an electronic format to the inmate. The mail itself is then destroyed by Reliance. It is never sent to the facility with the exception of any funds. Funds alone will be forwarded to the Portage County Jail by Reliance to be placed on the inmate’s account. Funds will not be accepted if sent directly to the Portage County Jail by friends or family members or any other entity. Where to Send Mail: Mail from the public should be sent to Reliance using the following address format. Portage County Jail – WI Inmate John Doe 1533 S 42 nd Street – Suite 200 Grand Forks, ND 58201 Important Mail Rules: All incoming and outgoing mail must have the full name and address of both the sender and the recipient on the envelope. The mail must have a valid postmark. Any money contained in the incoming mail will be placed in the inmate’s Stellar account. The inmate will receive a receipt. Any correspondence that does not comply with the Portage County Jail mail rules will not be uploaded from Mail Connect system, including mail that is deemed to be obscene or may compromise the security of the facility. Only hand-written or typed letters, cards, hand-colored drawings, and photos will be accepted. Pages may be no larger than 8.5" x 14" and a maximum of 15 sheets per day will be scanned. No staples. Originals will be destroyed by Reliance. See the Inmate Rules Handbook for more information related to incoming and outgoing inmate mail. Personal Property Only medications and money may be accepted at the Jail (guidelines apply). These items may be mailed or delivered by family/friends. Money may be deposited anytime in the Stellar Teller, and prescription medications may be dropped off anytime. View more information in the Inmate Rules Handbook .
