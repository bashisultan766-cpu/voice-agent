Our History The Hampton County Sheriff's Office in Hampton, South Carolina, has a rich history dating back to the formation of Hampton County. The office has played a crucial role in maintaining law and order within the county and upholding the safety and security of its residents. Hampton County was established in 1878, and the Sheriff's Office was likely established around the same time to enforce the law and serve the community. The office has evolved over the years to meet the changing needs of the county and its growing population. Sheriffs have played a significant role in the history of Hampton County. They have been responsible for upholding the law, investigating crimes, maintaining the county jail, and serving legal documents. Over the years, the Hampton County Sheriff's Office has adapted to new challenges, such as changes in law enforcement practices, advancements in technology, and shifts in the demographics and needs of the community. Throughout its history, the Hampton County Sheriff's Office has been involved in various notable events, investigations, and community outreach efforts. The office has worked to build relationships with the community, address issues such as crime prevention, and ensure the safety of Hampton County residents. Today, the Hampton County Sheriff's Office continues to serve the community by providing law enforcement services, ensuring public safety, and upholding the law. The office also collaborates with other local, state, and federal agencies to address modern challenges such as drug enforcement, cybercrime, and community policing.
