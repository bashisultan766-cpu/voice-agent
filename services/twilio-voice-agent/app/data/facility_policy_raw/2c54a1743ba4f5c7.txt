All non-legal inmate mail must be mailed to: Inmate Name and ID Number Yakima County WA PO Box 20888 Tampa, FL 33622 You may look up an inmates name and ID number on Inmate Lookup | Yakima County, WA . The inmate ID number must be six (6) digits. If the ID number contains fewer than six digits, add zeros before the other numbers. Legal Mail Legal mail may be sent directly to the jail at the address below. The envelope must clearly state “Legal Mail” in the bottom left or right corner and include: Inmate Name and Booking Number Yakima County Department of Corrections 111 N Front Street Yakima, WA 98901 Legal mail is defined as correspondence to or from the following: Courts or court staff An attorney of record in a filed local, state, or federal court case Established legal advocacy organizations (e.g., American Civil Liberties Union, Disability Rights of Washington, legal services groups, etc.) The President or Vice President of the United States Members of the United States Congress Embassies and Consulates United States Department of Justice Washington State Attorney General’s Office State Governors Members of State Legislatures Law Enforcement Officers acting in their official capacity Legal mail must be properly marked and identifiable on the exterior of the envelope. Acceptable Items Inmates may receive and possess the following mailed items: Books from a publisher Unacceptable Items The following items are not permitted: Greeting cards (including 3-D, electronic, pop-up, oversized, or embellished cards) Cardboard, staples, or rigid materials Stamps or stickers Any unknown substance (including hand-colored or painted drawings) Gang-related materials Polaroid photographs Bubble, padded, or hard envelopes Newspaper or magazine cutouts Booklets Photos, depictions, or writings that are: Gang-related or promote criminal activity Sexually explicit or contain nudity/partial nudity Racially inflammatory Detrimental to facility security Food items, perishables, or clothing Newspapers, periodicals, or books not sent directly from the publisher (Book Stores and Amazon are not publishers) Hardcover books Mail from any other inmate in this or another correctional facility Letters or packages without a return address Homemade postcards Mail containing coded messages or content reasonably believed to be coded Content that includes plans, instructions, or encouragement of criminal activity or rule violations Any item determined to be contraband or otherwise unauthorized by the Department Jeremy Welch Director Department of Corrections Email the Department 111 N Front Street Yakima, WA 98901 Phone: 509-574-1700 Visit Scheduling: 509-574-2929 Lobby Hours Monday - Friday 8 a.m. - 4:30 p.m. Staff Directory Agendas & Minutes ARPA Program Emergency Management Yakima County 128 North 2nd Street Yakima, WA 98901 Contact Us Alert Yakima Signup/Login Inmate Lookup Yakima County Employment Juvenile Justice Center Health Department /QuickLinks.aspx Home Site Map Accessibility Copyright Notices /QuickLinks.aspx Government Websites by CivicPlus® Loading Loading Do Not Show Again Close Arrow Left Arrow Right [] Slideshow Left Arrow Slideshow Right Arrow
