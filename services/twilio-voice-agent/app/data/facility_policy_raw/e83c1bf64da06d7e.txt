CLOSE Bio coming soon CLOSE Ernie Perez, Assistant Sheriff Ernie Perez is a lifelong resident of the Inland Empire, born in San Bernardino and raised in Grand Terrace, California. He was hired by the San Bernardino County Sheriff’s Department in 1995 and attended the 120th session of the Basic Academy. Ernie began his career as a deputy sheriff at the West Valley Detention Center before serving in patrol operations at Central Station. He later worked as an instructor at the Frank Bland Regional Training Center, teaching both the basic academy and firearms range. As a detective, he was assigned to the Narcotics Division. Throughout his career as a sergeant and lieutenant, Ernie served in custody, narcotics, and patrol assignments, including the West Valley Detention Center, Narcotics Division, and Rancho Cucamonga Patrol Station. As a captain, he commanded both the Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center and the Rancho Cucamonga Patrol Station. Ernie holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in Business Administration from California State University, Fullerton. He has completed numerous POST leadership programs, including the Sherman Block Supervisory Leadership Institute (Class #357) and the Management and Executive Development Course. He is also a graduate of the Inland Empire Economic Partnership Regional Leadership Academy and the San Bernardino County Management and Leadership Academy’s Executive Development Program. In March 2023, Ernie was promoted to Deputy Chief, overseeing the Administration and Planning Bureau, Desert Patrol Bureau, and Valley/Mountains Patrol Bureau. In October 2025, Sheriff Shannon Dicus promoted him to Assistant Sheriff. Ernie currently oversees the Department’s Support Operations. CLOSE Edward Bachman, Deputy Chief Edward Bachman is a long-term resident of San Bernardino County. He began his career with the San Bernardino County Sheriff’s Department in 2002 and attended the 150th session of the Basic Academy. Edward began his career as a deputy sheriff at the West Valley Detention Center and later at the Apple Valley Station. As a detective, he was assigned to the Apple Valley Station and the Specialized Investigations Division, Homicide Detail. As a sergeant, he served at the High Desert Detention Center, Apple Valley Station, and the Professional Standards Division – Internal Affairs Unit. As a lieutenant, he was assigned to the Professional Standards Division – Internal Affairs Unit and the Victorville Station. As a captain, Edward served as the commander of the Glen Helen Rehabilitation Center and the Information Services Division. Edward holds numerous certificates from the California Commission on Peace Officer Standards and Training, including his POST Management Certificate. He earned a Bachelor of Science in Management from California Coast University and a Master of Arts in Organizational Leadership from California Baptist University. He is also a graduate of the Sherman Block Supervisory Leadership Institute, Class 420, and the Executive Leadership Institute at Claremont Graduate University, Drucker School of Management, Class 8. Edward is a member of the International Association of Chiefs of Police and the California Police Chiefs Association. Sheriff Shannon D. Dicus promoted Edward to Deputy Chief in November of 2025. As Deputy Chief, he oversees the Field Support Services Bureau, which includes the Information Services Division, Coroner/Public Administrator Division, and the Scientific Investigations Division.
